The Universal Reading List Project

For several years, I've been trying to get the most word-for-word value out of my reading.

Of course, there are many types of merit a book can have: practical value, cultural import, sheer pleasure, etc. And the benefits are subjective and individual. Nevertheless, certain books bubble to the top. They're consistently deemed worth reading by people who spend much more time thinking about literary merit than I do.

In the spirit of not letting the perfect be the enemy of the good, I created a book list and got to work reading. Even though I'm sure my list is deeply flawed, I've learned so much more than I would have if I'd never tried to systematize my consumption.

This site is my attempt to expand the projects to all types of consumable media and experiences. In the future, I'd like to expand the database, craft more objective standards of merit, subject all materials to expert review, and offer transparent information so that you can decide for yourself what's worth your time.
