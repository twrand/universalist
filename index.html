The Universal Reading List Project

This project is devoted to the ideal of getting the most word-for-word, minute-for-minute, note-for-note value out of your consumption, where consumption means reading, viewing, listening, and experiencing the world in all its wonder.

Wait, what? Should we care about about cultural value, historical importance, technical merit, factuality, insight, beauty, applications, or something else? (Yes.) Are all of these qualities subjective, culturally relative, and historically arbitrary? (Yes, but not to the point of meaninglessness, unless you prefer to make major epistemic concessions.)

The goal of this project is to curate lists of media worth experiencing according to a transparent methodology, in the spirit of not letting the perfect be the enemy of the good. Future goals include expanding the database, crafting more objective standards of merit, subjecting all materials to expert review, and making the results more accessible.
